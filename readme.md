📂 Category Map

These seven categories reflect how I think about AI architecture at enterprise scale — not by technology silo, but by solution archetype. Each represents a recurring pattern I've encountered in real-world engagements, organized around a common question a business stakeholder might ask.

| # | Category | The Business Question It Answers |
|---|----------|----------------------------------|
| 01 | **Enterprise RAG Systems** | *"Our knowledge is scattered across documents and databases — how do we make it searchable and actionable?"* |
| 02 | **Multi-Agent Orchestration** | *"We have complex workflows that need research, validation, and synthesis — can AI handle the coordination?"* |
| 03 | **AI + Automation Integration** | *"We already have RPA/BPM in place — how do we layer AI on top without rebuilding everything?"* |
| 04 | **Voice & Conversational AI** | *"We need to scale conversations — tutoring, support, coaching — what does the architecture look like?"* |
| 05 | **Process Intelligence & Analytics** | *"Our processes are black boxes — how do we mine, monitor, and predict bottlenecks?"* |
| 06 | **Secure AI Infrastructure** | *"How do we expose AI capabilities internally without compromising security, compliance, or audit?"* |
| 07 | **Domain-Specific AI** | *"How do we adapt general-purpose AI to regulated, specialized domains like healthcare or finance?"* |

The categories are intentionally cross-cutting. A single solution often spans multiple archetypes — for example, a healthcare assistant (Domain-Specific) might use RAG (Enterprise RAG) behind a secure API gateway (Secure AI Infrastructure). The portfolio is designed to be mix-and-match: grab the patterns relevant to your problem and compose them.

🧩 Design Philosophy

**Business Alignment** → Every design ties back to KPIs (efficiency, compliance, retention, innovation).

**Modularity** → Reusable components (data pipelines, model lifecycle, observability).

**Governance** → Bias detection, explainability, audit trails, regulatory readiness.

**Scalability** → Cloud-native, containerized, CI/CD pipelines.

**Observability** → Drift detection, monitoring, dashboards for transparency.
